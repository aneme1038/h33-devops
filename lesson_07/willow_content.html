Willow

Willow (Salix) is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs in the family Salicaceae, native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. The genus includes around 400 species, ranging from small shrubs to large trees.

Willows typically have slender, flexible branches and narrow leaves. Many species grow near water and are known for their rapid growth. Some well-known species include the weeping willow (Salix babylonica) and the white willow (Salix alba).
